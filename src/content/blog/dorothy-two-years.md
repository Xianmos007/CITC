## The first thing we learned

We thought the work would be mostly about the mailbox post. It was leaning toward the street, the old wood had gone soft at the base, and Ms. Dorothy had been waiting for someone with a post-hole digger and a free Saturday.

What we found, after a few weekends, was that the repair was only the doorway. The real work was learning how to come back without making a scene.

> Seek the welfare of the city where I have sent you, and pray to the Lord on its behalf.

By the end of the second year, the list was longer than the mailbox:

- A shed roof patched before the summer rain
- A side gate rehung after a storm
- Yard bags filled slowly, one humid morning at a time

The surprising part was how ordinary it all became. Someone brought coffee. Someone brought gloves. Someone remembered that Ms. Dorothy likes the porch swept before the tools are packed.

**Showing up is not complicated.** It is just hard to keep doing without needing it to become impressive.

That may be the gift of these Saturdays. They keep us close enough to the ground to remember what love usually looks like.
